## User Story

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **User story** | **As** a Manulife agent (FA) using M-Smart, **I want** to describe my customer's full family in a single natural-language message and have M-Smart correctly identify who is the Policy Owner (PO), who is the Main Insured (MI), and who are the Other Insured (OI — spouse/children), asking me back only for what's genuinely missing, **So that** I don't have to fill in separate forms for each family member and the rest of the Quick Pitch flow starts from an accurate, agent-confirmed family profile. |
| **Actor:** | Manulife Agent (FA) |
| **Trigger:** | FA sends a free-text message describing 2 or more family members (self + spouse and/or children) inside an active M-Smart Quick Pitch conversation |
| **Pre-condition:** | FA is licensed (holds a valid MIT certification per MGAFS-1834); M-Smart session is active |
| **Post-condition:** | A structured, agent-confirmed **Family Profile** (PO, MI, up to 3 OI, each with age/gender/relationship, plus PO's income/budget, risk appetite, payment term) is stored in session state and made available to the Recommendation capability (US-002) |

## 1. Scope

- **In scope:**
  - Extracting PO, MI, and up to 3 OI (spouse + children) from one natural-language Vietnamese message.
  - Resolving Vietnamese relationship keywords (vợ / chồng / con / ba / mẹ / cha / cô / dì / chú / bác).
  - Determining PO vs. MI (default PO = MI unless an explicit signal states otherwise).
  - Conversational ask-back for missing mandatory fields (max 2–3 fields per turn, never re-asking).
  - Role Confirmation Card when PO ≠ MI, requiring explicit FA confirmation before any downstream step.
  - Enforcing the 3-OI-per-policy limit with a graceful prioritization prompt.
  - Blocking a minor (<18) PO while preserving already-collected MI/OI data.
  - Age-anchored eligibility resolution rules for the PO≠MI role combinations (husband/wife, parent/child,
    adult-child/elderly-parent).
  - Default occupation class (=1 adult, =2 child <18) with silent auto-apply, and accepting an
    FA-volunteered class 4 declaration with a non-blocking warning.
  - FA license gate (MIT) enforced before any Quick Pitch processing begins.
- **Out of scope:**
  - Extended family beyond spouse and children (parents, siblings as insured members).
  - Auto-fetching customer data from CRM/DPMS — extraction is based solely on the conversational input.
  - Product/rider recommendation content itself — belongs to **US-002**.
  - Premium calculation — belongs to **US-003**.
  - Illustration PDF generation — belongs to **US-004**.
  - Saving the family profile to CRM or any customer record.
  - English-language input/output.

## 2. Requirement

#### R.01 Multi-Entity Family Extraction from Natural Language

Parse one free-text agent message and extract every mentioned family member's role, age/date of birth,
and gender, per the **Mandatory Information Matrix**:

| Field | PO | MI | OI-1 (Spouse) | OI-2/3 (Child) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Date of birth / Age | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory |
| Gender | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory |
| Occupation class (1–4) | Default = 1 | Default = 1 | Default = 1 | Default = 2 if < 18 |
| Income / Budget | Mandatory | — | — | — |
| Risk appetite, payment term | Mandatory | — | — | — |
| Relationship to PO | — | Conditional (only if PO ≠ MI) | Mandatory (Spouse) | Mandatory (Child) |
| Is PO also MI? | Conditional | — | — | — |
| Full name | Optional | Optional | Optional | Optional |

Sample: *"Khách hàng Nam, 34 tuổi, ngân sách 50 triệu/tháng, tiết kiệm, đóng phí 10 năm, khẩu vị rủi ro
cao. Vợ 30 tuổi. Con trai 4 tuổi."* → PO=MI (Nam, 34t), OI-1 = Vợ (Nữ, 30t), OI-2 = Con (Nam, 4t), budget
50tr/tháng, mục tiêu tiết kiệm, đóng phí 10 năm, rủi ro cao.

#### R.02 Conversational Ask-Back for Missing Mandatory Fields

When one or more mandatory `(B)` fields from the matrix above are missing, M-Smart must not hard-block
with a technical error. It asks back conversationally, grouping at most 2–3 missing fields per turn, in
priority order **PO → MI → OI**, and never re-asks a field already provided.

#### R.03 Role Confirmation Card (PO ≠ MI)

When the message implies PO and MI are different people (e.g. *"Bố mua bảo hiểm cho con"*), M-Smart
displays a Role Confirmation Card and must receive explicit FA confirmation ("Đúng" / "Chỉnh sửa") before
any product eligibility calculation runs downstream.

#### R.04 Vietnamese Relationship Keyword Resolution

Resolve family relationship terms — **vợ / chồng / con / ba / mẹ / cha / cô / dì / chú / bác** — including
nested references (e.g. "chồng con gái" = the PO's own son-in-law, not the MI's), and ask for
clarification when a mentioned person's relationship is ambiguous (e.g. "another person, 25 years old").

#### R.05 Family Composition Constraints

- Enforce a hard limit of 3 OI per policy; if the FA describes more, politely inform them and ask which
  members to prioritize.
- If the confirmed PO is under 18, immediately block Quick Pitch generation (before any product call),
  while preserving all already-collected MI/OI data so the FA only has to re-supply PO information.

#### R.06 Age-Anchored Eligibility Resolution by Role Combination

- **PO = husband, MI = wife:** eligibility/coverage-term parameters are anchored to MI's (wife's) age and
  gender; PO's age is validated only against PO-specific constraints (PO ≥ 18; PO's own max issue age).
- **PO = parent, MI = child (0–17):** juvenile-eligible products only; illustration occupancy code is set
  to 2 (per MGAFS-2066); riders with a minimum issue age above the child's current age are filtered out;
  a child under 30 days old blocks the entire pitch.
- **PO = adult child, MI = elderly parent:** MI's age determines eligibility; long-horizon ILP products
  are filtered out once MI exceeds their max issue age, with elder-appropriate (health/medical) products
  recommended instead; if MI's age exceeds the max issue age for **every** available product, no pitch is
  generated.

#### R.07 FA License Gate

Before any Family Quick Pitch processing starts, verify the FA holds a valid MIT certification per the
existing MGAFS-1834 standard — no exception for the multi-persona flow versus the existing single-persona
flow.

## 3. Business Rules & Validations

| Rule ID | Rule Name | Business Description | Impacted flow | Example Scenario |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BR-US001-01 | PO = MI by default | The system assumes PO = MI unless the message contains an explicit signal that a different person pays vs. is covered. Refer to AC1, AC5 | create (intake) | "Khách hàng Nam 38t... muốn mua cho cả 2 vợ chồng" → PO=MI=Nam; no explicit PO≠MI signal |
| BR-US001-02 | Occupation class default, never proactively asked | Class defaults to 1 for adult PO/MI/OI and 2 for a child OI < 18; the system never asks about occupation. Refer to AC6 | create (intake) | No occupation mentioned → class silently defaults; FA is never prompted |
| BR-US001-03 | Occupation class 4 accepted only if volunteered | Class 4 is only processed when the FA proactively states it; triggers a non-blocking warning. Refer to AC6 | create (intake) | FA states "nghề nghiệp hạng 4 cho vợ" → accepted with warning, flow continues |
| BR-US001-04 | Max 3 OI per policy | A policy supports at most 3 Other Insured; excess members trigger a prioritization prompt, not silent truncation. Refer to AC7 | create (intake) | Spouse + 2 children + 1 more child mentioned → system asks FA to prioritize 3 of the 4 |
| BR-US001-05 | PO must be ≥ 18 | A PO under 18 is a hard block; MI/OI data already collected is preserved. Refer to AC11 | create (intake) | "17 tuổi sắp 18" is still treated as 17 → blocked; asks for exact date of birth |
| BR-US001-06 | MI's age governs product eligibility when PO ≠ MI | Eligibility, coverage term, and premium term parameters use MI's age/gender; PO's age is checked only against PO-specific limits. ## 4. Impact and Risk

1. **Backend:** New/extended multi-entity NLP extraction prompt; session-state schema extended to hold a
   multi-persona family object (PO + MI + up to 3 OI) shared with US-002/US-003/US-004.
2. **Security/Privacy risk:** none beyond the existing session data handling — no new persistent storage of
   PII is introduced (CRM save is explicitly out of scope).
3. **Accuracy risk:** Vietnamese pronoun collision (e.g. "em" meaning "younger sibling of the MI" and not
   the FA) and nested relationship graphs (e.g. "chồng con gái") are the highest-severity parsing failure
   modes identified in the source scenarios (Group B) — see AC4 and AC5 negative/edge coverage.
4. **Regression risk:** the existing single-persona quick pitch intake path must continue to work unchanged
   for a single-person message.

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements

1. **Accuracy:** LLM role/field extraction must achieve ≥ 95% correct PO/MI/OI identification on a
   50-scenario Vietnamese test set before this story is considered done.
2. **Conversational economy:** ask-back must never request more than 2–3 fields per turn and must never
   re-request information already supplied in the conversation.
3. **Language:** all new prompts and messages are Vietnamese-only.
4. **Audit/Logging:** the resolved Family Profile (including which fields were defaulted vs. explicitly
   confirmed) is logged per turn for later traceability into US-002/US-003.

## 6. Dependencies / References

1. FA License Check (MGAFS-2071) — in progress; License Gate (R.07) integrates with it, no rebuild.
2. Rider Eligibility Matrix (min/max age, gender restrictions per rider/product) — being authored by BA
   into the knowledge base (Pre-Sprint Blocker #2); required so R.06's filtering decisions are correct,
   although the matrix itself is consumed primarily by **US-002**.
3. Downstream: US-002 (Product & Rider Recommendation) consumes the Family Profile object produced here.
4. MGAFS-2066 (occupancy code rules) for the juvenile-MI occupancy=2 rule in R.06.

## 7. Technical Note

1. The Family Profile object is the sole contract handed to US-002; US-002 must not need to re-parse the
   original free-text message.
2. Occupation class handling is intentionally passive (never asked) — do not add a proactive occupation
   question in any future iteration without a PO decision, per BR-US001-02.
3. Relationship parsing must resolve nested references (e.g. "chồng con gái") to the correct entity in the
   family graph — recommend unit tests built directly from source scenarios B1–B3 in
   `user-story-family-quick-pitch.html`.

## 8. Acceptance Criteria

#### AC1. [Happy Path] Full mandatory information provided → Family Profile confirmed

**Given** the FA sends one message that provides every mandatory `(B)` field for PO, MI, and each OI mentioned (age/DOB, gender, relationship to PO, and for PO: income/budget, risk appetite, payment term)  
**When** M-Smart processes the message  
**Then** it displays a confirmation card summarizing the understood family structure (role, age, gender per member) and the PO's budget/goal/payment term, and stores the confirmed Family Profile in session state  
**And** it does **not** proceed to recommendation until the FA confirms or edits the card.

#### AC2. [Happy Path] Multiple OI displayed as distinct, correctly-ordered members

**Given** the FA describes PO/MI + spouse + 2 children (3 OI total) in one message, including a child whose age is given in months  
**When** M-Smart builds the Family Profile  
**Then** it creates exactly 3 distinct OI entries labeled "NĐBH Phụ 1/2/3" with the correct role (Vợ/Chồng vs. Con), and the child's age-in-months is stored as a decimal age (e.g. 8 tháng → 0.67)  
**And** each OI is validated independently — a validation issue on one OI does not block the others.

#### AC3. [Validation] Missing mandatory field → conversational ask-back, not a hard error

**Given** the FA's message omits a mandatory field (e.g. a child's age, or the PO's budget)  
**When** M-Smart detects the gap  
**Then** it asks a natural-language follow-up grouping at most 2–3 missing fields, in priority order PO → MI → OI (e.g. *"Để đề xuất quyền lợi phù hợp nhất cho con của anh/chị, em cần biết thêm: bé bao nhiêu tuổi và là bé trai hay bé gái ạ?"*)  
**And** once the FA answers, M-Smart does not re-ask for information already provided  
**And** M-Smart never asks about occupation proactively.

#### AC4. [Edge Case] Ambiguous relationship → clarification required before proceeding

**Given** the FA mentions a person without stating their relationship to the PO (e.g. *"một người nữa, 25 tuổi"*)  
**When** M-Smart parses the message  
**Then** it asks *"Người 25 tuổi là vợ/chồng hay con của khách hàng ạ?"*  
**And** only adds this person to the Family Profile once the role is confirmed.

#### AC5. [Edge Case] PO ≠ MI → Role Confirmation Card is mandatory before continuing

**Given** the message implies PO and MI are different people (e.g. *"Chồng tôi 40 tuổi muốn bảo vệ cho con 8 tuổi"*)  
**When** M-Smart detects the PO ≠ MI pattern  
**Then** it displays a Role Confirmation Card stating the PO (payer) and MI (main insured, with their relationship to PO) and asks the FA to confirm  
**And** does **not** proceed to eligibility/recommendation until the FA confirms or edits the card  
**And** if the sentence is itself ambiguous about who is the main insured, asks explicitly: *"Ai sẽ là NĐBH chính — bản thân anh/chị hay thành viên khác?"*

#### AC6. [Edge Case] FA volunteers occupation class 4 → accepted with warning, not blocked

**Given** the FA proactively states occupation class 4 for the MI or an OI  
**When** M-Smart builds the recommendation-ready profile  
**Then** it overrides the default class (1) with class 4 and shows a non-blocking warning: *"⚠️ Nghề nghiệp hạng 4 — một số sản phẩm đính kèm có thể không áp dụng và cần xem xét thêm tại khâu thẩm định."*  
**And** the flow continues — this is advisory only.

#### AC7. [Edge Case] More than 3 OI mentioned → limit enforced with a prioritization prompt

**Given** the FA's message describes more than 3 Other Insured in a single request  
**When** M-Smart detects the overflow  
**Then** it informs the FA that a family policy supports at most 3 Other Insured, proposes the first 3 by default, and asks: *"anh/chị muốn ưu tiên ai ạ?"*  
**And** lets the FA re-select which members are included.

#### AC8. [Edge Case] PO = husband, MI = wife → eligibility anchored to MI's age

**Given** PO = husband (age + gender confirmed) and MI = wife (age + gender confirmed)  
**When** M-Smart finalizes the profile for handoff to recommendation  
**Then** it flags MI's (wife's) age/gender as the primary eligibility parameters, and PO's age as validated only against PO-specific constraints (PO ≥ 18; PO max age per product)  
**And** if the husband's age exceeds the maximum PO age for the intended product, the pitch is blocked with a notice to choose a different product or update PO information.

#### AC9. [Edge Case] PO = parent, MI = child → juvenile plan constraints applied

**Given** PO = parent (≥18) and MI = child (0–17)  
**When** M-Smart finalizes the profile  
**Then** it flags the profile for juvenile-eligible products only, sets occupancy = 2 (per MGAFS-2066), and filters out riders whose minimum issue age exceeds the child's current age  
**And** if the child is under 30 days old, blocks the entire pitch with *"Bé chưa đủ tuổi tham gia bảo hiểm (tối thiểu 30 ngày tuổi)."*

#### AC10. [Negative Case] PO = adult child, MI = elderly parent — no eligible product exists

**Given** PO = adult child (≥18) and MI = an elderly parent whose age exceeds the maximum issue age for every available product  
**When** M-Smart evaluates eligibility for handoff to recommendation  
**Then** it does **not** generate any pitch and responds: *"Hiện không có sản phẩm phù hợp. Vui lòng liên hệ chuyên gia tư vấn."*  
**And** if only some products are excluded (not all), it instead flags elder-appropriate (health/medical) products as eligible and notes the exclusion transparently to the FA.

#### AC11. [Negative Case] PO is a minor — hard block with data preservation

**Given** a PO ≠ MI scenario where the confirmed PO age is under 18  
**When** M-Smart evaluates the profile  
**Then** it immediately blocks Quick Pitch generation — no downstream product call is made — and displays: *"Người mua bảo hiểm (BMBH) phải đủ 18 tuổi. BMBH [X tuổi] chưa đủ điều kiện. Vui lòng cung cấp thông tin BMBH hợp lệ."*  
**And** preserves all already-collected MI/OI profile data so the FA only re-enters PO information  
**And** if the FA states "17 tuổi sắp 18", treats it as age 17 (still blocked) and asks for the exact date of birth to confirm eligibility.

#### AC12. [Exception Case] FA lacks a valid MIT license — flow blocked before any processing

**Given** the FA does not hold a valid MIT certification  
**When** the FA attempts to start a Family Quick Pitch conversation  
**Then** M-Smart blocks the flow using the existing MGAFS-1834 messaging standard  
**And** there is no exception for the multi-persona flow versus the existing single-persona flow.

#### AC13. [Integration Failure] Low-confidence extraction → ask-back instead of a silent guess

**Given** the NLP extraction step returns a low-confidence or conflicting role assignment for any family member (e.g. the model cannot determine whether "em" refers to the agent or a sibling of the MI)  
**When** M-Smart evaluates the extraction result  
**Then** it must **not** silently assume a role — it asks the FA to clarify the specific ambiguous reference before adding that member to the Family Profile  
**And** the rest of the already-confidently-extracted profile is not discarded while waiting for the clarification.
