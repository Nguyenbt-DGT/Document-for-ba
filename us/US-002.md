## User Story

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **User story** | **As** a Manulife agent (FA) using M-Smart, **I want** M-Smart to recommend the right base product for the main insured and the right riders for each spouse/child in the family — respecting any product or rider I explicitly specify myself — **So that** I receive one coherent, itemized family package instead of manually looking up eligibility and building each member's coverage by hand. |
| **Actor:** | Manulife Agent (FA) |
| **Trigger:** | A confirmed **Family Profile** is available from US-001 (Family Intake & Role Resolution) |
| **Pre-condition:** | US-001 has produced a validated Family Profile (PO, MI, up to 3 OI with age/gender/relationship confirmed) |
| **Post-condition:** | A structured **Recommended Package** (base product for MI + itemized rider list per OI, with any agent-directed overrides applied and labeled) is stored in session state and handed to US-003 for pricing |

2. **Screen demo:** N/A — output is a conversational (chat) message described in the Acceptance Criteria;
   M-Smart is a Gen AI chatbot, so the recommendation is always presented in chat, never a UI card.

## 1. Scope

- **In scope:**
  - Selecting the base product for PO/MI based on goal, risk appetite, and payment term.
  - Selecting at least one eligible rider per OI (spouse, each child) where eligibility allows.
  - Correctly scoping per-person product/rider exclusions to the exact person named.
  - Continuing to build a valid partial package when no eligible rider exists for one member, rather than
    blocking the whole pitch.
  - Treating each child as an independent rider profile, including same-age (twin) children as separate
    line items.
  - Accepting and validating agent-directed overrides: named rider types, explicit coverage amounts
    (including Vietnamese numeric shorthand such as "200k/ngày"), named base products/plan codes, and
    explicit rider exclusions (with dependency-conflict checking).
  - Assembling the final itemized package object handed to the Budget/Premium capability (US-003).
- **Out of scope:**
  - Parsing the original free-text message or resolving PO/MI/OI roles — belongs to **US-001**.
  - Any premium/APE calculation or budget-fit trimming — belongs to **US-003**.
  - Illustration PDF rendering — belongs to **US-004**.
  - AI/ML-based (non-deterministic) product selection — eligibility and matching must be rule-based
    against each product/rider's existing validation rules: ePOS validation rules, or the official
    product documentation (provision document / product spec).
  - Separate independent policies per family member (single consolidated policy only).

## 2. Requirement

#### R.01 Base Product Recommendation for PO/MI

Given the confirmed Family Profile (goal, risk appetite, payment term, PO/MI age & gender), select the
base product that best matches, applying the age-anchoring rules resolved by US-001 (e.g. MI's age governs
when PO ≠ MI).

> 🧑‍💼 **Ví dụ thực tế:** PO/MI = chồng 34t, mục tiêu "tiết kiệm", khẩu vị rủi ro cao, đóng phí 18 năm →
> M-Smart chọn một sản phẩm nền hướng tiết kiệm/đầu tư phù hợp (ví dụ dòng ILP) thay vì một sản phẩm bảo
> vệ thuần túy, vì mục tiêu và khẩu vị rủi ro của khách hàng khớp với nhóm sản phẩm đó.

#### R.02 Per-OI Rider Recommendation

For each OI (spouse, each child independently), select at least one eligible rider where product/age/
gender eligibility allows, based on each product/rider's existing validation rules: ePOS validation
rules, or the official product documentation (provision document / product spec).

> 🧑‍💼 **Ví dụ thực tế:** gia đình có vợ 31t và cặp song sinh 3 tuổi → M-Smart đề xuất riêng một rider bảo
> vệ (ví dụ CI) cho vợ, và riêng cho từng bé một rider phù hợp độ tuổi (ví dụ HCB) — mỗi người được xét
> điều kiện độc lập, không dùng chung một gói cho cả 3 người.

#### R.03 Narrowly-Scoped Product/Rider Exclusions

When the FA states an exclusion (e.g. *"không cần ILP cho mẹ"*), apply it only to the exact person named —
never generalize it to any other family member, even one with a similar product need.

#### R.04 Eligibility Gap Handling

If no eligible rider exists for a given OI (product incompatibility, age restriction, etc.), do not block
the overall recommendation — inform the FA and continue building the package for the remaining members.

> 🧑‍💼 **Ví dụ thực tế:** sản phẩm nền được chọn cho chồng không có rider nào tương thích cho vợ (do khác
> dòng sản phẩm) → M-Smart vẫn trả về gói đầy đủ cho chồng và các con, đồng thời báo rõ: *"Hiện không có
> rider phù hợp cho vợ/chồng với sản phẩm đã chọn"* — thay vì dừng cả pitch lại.

#### R.05 Multiple Children Handling

Treat each child as a separate insured with an independently calculated rider set. Same-age (twin)
children are rendered as two distinct line items, never collapsed into one doubled item.

> 🧑‍💼 **Ví dụ thực tế:** "*cặp song sinh 3 tuổi 1 trai 1 gái*" → gói đề xuất có **hai dòng riêng biệt**
> (Con — song sinh 1, Con — song sinh 2), mỗi dòng tự tính điều kiện/rider riêng, không gộp thành một dòng
> "2 con x rider" vì hai bé có thể đủ/không đủ điều kiện khác nhau dù cùng tuổi.

#### R.06 Consolidated Family Pitch Assembly

Assemble the base product (PO/MI) and every member's rider list into one itemized **Recommended Package**
object — the sole contract handed to US-003 for pricing.

> 🧑‍💼 **Ví dụ thực tế:** sản phẩm nền Xanh Tương Lai (chồng) + rider CI (vợ) + rider HCB (con) → được gộp
> thành **một** gói minh họa duy nhất chuyển sang bước tính phí (US-003), chứ không phải 3 báo giá rời rạc
> cho từng người mà FA phải tự cộng lại.

#### R.07 Agent-Directed Rider Control

Recognize explicit rider assignments per member in Vietnamese or English (e.g. *"CI rider cho vợ, health
rider cho con"* — CI/bệnh hiểm nghèo, health/y tế, accident/tai nạn, HCB/trợ cấp nằm viện), bind each named
rider to the specified member after validating eligibility, and label it *"theo yêu cầu"* to distinguish it
from system-suggested items.

#### R.08 Agent-Directed Coverage Amounts

Parse an explicit sum assured using Vietnamese number conventions (e.g. *"500 triệu"* = 500,000,000 VND;
*"200k/ngày"* = 200,000 VND/day), confirm the interpretation with the FA before applying it, validate
against the rider's min/max sum assured for the member's age, and use the agent-stated amount as-is —
never silently substitute a calculated default.

#### R.09 Agent-Directed Base Product Selection

Accept an explicitly named base product or plan code instead of running the default recommendation
algorithm, after validating: (1) the product is currently active, (2) the FA holds the required license,
(3) the product is eligible for MI's age/profile. If discontinued, propose the nearest active replacement
and ask the FA to confirm before continuing.

#### R.10 Agent-Directed Rider Exclusion

When the FA requests a rider be removed, check for dependency conflicts first (e.g. an accident rider that
is a prerequisite for a Waiver of Premium rider) and warn before removing; after confirmation, remove it,
recalculate the package, and never silently re-add it later in the same session. If the rider is mandatory
for the chosen base product, block the exclusion and explain why.

## 3. Business Rules & Validations

| Rule ID | Rule Name | Business Description | Impacted flow | Example Scenario |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BR-US002-01 | Exclusions are person-scoped, never generalized | A stated product/rider exclusion for one family member must not be applied to any other member. Refer to AC7 | create (recommendation) | "không cần ILP cho mẹ" (MI) must not remove ILP eligibility from OI-1 (husband), who still wants savings |
| BR-US002-02 | Dual product intent on one policy | A single policy carries the base product (e.g. ILP) for MI plus protection-only riders for each OI — never a separate base policy per OI. Refer to AC1, AC2 | create (recommendation) | ILP base for the husband + protection-only riders for wife and infant, on one policy |
| BR-US002-03 | Missing rider eligibility does not block the pitch | If no rider is eligible for a member, exclude that member's coverage and continue with the rest of the package. Refer to AC4 | create (recommendation) | No eligible rider for a spouse due to product incompatibility → package still generated for MI + children |
| BR-US002-04 | Below-minimum-age child is excluded, not blocking | A child younger than every available rider's minimum entry age is dropped from the package; the rest of the family package proceeds. Refer to AC5 | create (recommendation) | Newborn under 30 days → excluded from riders this round; rest of family package unaffected |
| BR-US002-05 | Numeric shorthand parsing | "k" = nghìn (thousand) VND; "triệu"/"tr" = million VND. Misreading magnitude is a critical parsing failure. Refer to AC9 | create (recommendation) | "HCB 200k/ngày" = 200,000 VND/day, not 200 VND or 200,000,000 VND |
| BR-US002-06 | Agent-stated values are authoritative | Any agent-specified rider, amount, or product is used exactly as stated (after eligibility validation) — never silently replaced with a system default. Refer to AC8, AC9, AC10 | create (recommendation) | FA states "CI 500 triệu cho vợ" → used as 500,000,000 VND, not recalculated to a different suggested amount |
| BR-US002-07 | Rider dependency check before exclusion | A rider cannot be removed if another active rider depends on it, without an explicit warning and confirmation. Refer to AC11 | update (recommendation) | Removing the accident rider that a WOP rider depends on triggers a warning before removal |

## 4. Impact and Risk

1. **Backend:** New eligibility-matching/recommendation-builder service consuming the Family Profile
   (from US-001) and each product/rider's existing validation rules — ePOS validation rules, or the
   official product documentation (provision document / product spec); produces the Recommended Package
   object.
2. **Integration risk:** the recommendation engine must read min/max age, gender, and product compatibility
   directly from each product's existing ePOS validation rules, or the official product documentation
   (provision document / product spec) — SA must confirm the recommendation engine can query these
   existing sources before dev starts; no new consolidated eligibility matrix is authored for this story.
3. **Scope-leak risk:** BR-US002-01 (person-scoped exclusion) is the single highest-risk business rule in
   this capability — must be enforced in code/logic, not left to prompt discipline; requires the explicit
   negative-case AC7 below.
4. **Regression risk:** the existing single-persona recommendation path must continue to work unchanged.

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements

1. **Determinism:** rider/product eligibility matching must be rule-based against each product's existing
   validation rules — ePOS validation rules, or the official product documentation (provision document /
   product spec) — not an AI/ML guess — the same Family Profile input must always produce the same
   Recommended Package.
2. **Language:** all recommendation labels and FA-facing confirmations are Vietnamese.
3. **Traceability:** every item in the Recommended Package must be tagged as either system-suggested or
   "theo yêu cầu" (agent-directed) for downstream display in US-003/US-004.

## 6. Dependencies / References

1. **US-001 (Family Intake & Role Resolution)** — upstream, provides the confirmed Family Profile.
2. **Per-product validation rules** — ePOS validation rules, or the official product documentation
   (provision document / product spec) — min/max age, gender, product compatibility per rider code;
   already maintained by the Product/Underwriting team; the recommendation engine integrates with these
   existing sources rather than a new matrix.
3. **RUV14 Base Product Setup (MGAFS-2057)** — in progress; include RUV14 riders once complete.
4. **Product Engine API — multi-insured support** (Pre-Sprint Blocker #1) — shared risk with US-003;
   confirms whether eligibility/product lookups can be batched per family or must be sequential per member.
5. Downstream: **US-003 (Budget Allocation & Premium Calculation)** consumes the Recommended Package.

## 7. Technical Note

1. The Recommended Package object must carry, per line item, enough metadata (member ID, product/rider
   code, requested sum assured, source = system/agent) for US-003 to price it and for US-004 to render it
   without re-deriving business logic.
2. Twin/same-age children must be modeled as two independent line items at the data-model level, not a
   quantity-2 flag on one line item, to avoid downstream rendering bugs in US-004.
3. Discontinued-product substitution (R.09) should reuse the existing single-persona product-swap logic if
   one already exists, rather than building a parallel implementation.

## 8. Acceptance Criteria

#### AC1. [Happy Path] Complete package recommended for a straightforward family

**Given** a confirmed Family Profile with PO=MI, one spouse, and one child, all eligible  
**When** M-Smart builds the recommendation  
**Then** it returns a base product for PO/MI matched to goal/risk/term, at least one rider for the spouse, and at least one rider for the child  
**And** every line item is tagged as system-suggested and ready for pricing handoff to US-003.

#### AC2. [Happy Path] Consolidated itemized package assembled correctly

**Given** a confirmed Family Profile with PO/MI + spouse + 2 children  
**When** M-Smart assembles the Recommended Package  
**Then** the package contains one base-product line (PO/MI) and one rider line per OI, each individually identified by member and rider code, ready to be priced independently by US-003.

#### AC3. [Validation] Twin children are rendered as two independent line items

**Given** the Family Profile contains two children of the same age (twins)  
**When** M-Smart builds their rider recommendations  
**Then** it produces **two separate** line items, one per child, each independently eligible-checked — never a single doubled-quantity line item.

#### AC4. [Edge Case] No eligible rider for a member → package continues without blocking

**Given** no available rider is eligible for the spouse (product incompatibility, age restriction, etc.)  
**When** M-Smart finalizes the recommendation  
**Then** it does **not** block the overall package — it informs the FA (*"Hiện không có rider phù hợp cho vợ/chồng với sản phẩm đã chọn. Gói đề xuất sẽ không bao gồm bảo hiểm cho vợ/chồng."*) and returns the valid remaining package for MI and the other OI.

#### AC5. [Edge Case] Child below minimum insurable age is excluded, not blocking

**Given** a child's age is below the minimum required by every available child rider  
**When** M-Smart evaluates that child's eligibility  
**Then** it excludes the child from the package, informs the FA clearly, and continues generating the package for the remaining family members.

#### AC6. [Edge Case] Multiple children with different ages, calculated independently

**Given** the Family Profile has 2+ children of different ages  
**When** M-Smart builds rider recommendations  
**Then** each child is treated as a separate insured with independently calculated rider eligibility  
**And** the package is annotated so that if a later budget constraint (US-003) requires deprioritizing a child, the youngest child is deprioritized first — this story only produces the ranked/annotated package, the actual trimming decision is executed by US-003.

#### AC7. [Negative Case] Person-scoped exclusion must never generalize to the family

**Given** the FA states a product exclusion for exactly one named member (e.g. *"không cần ILP cho mẹ"* where "mẹ" is the MI), while another member (e.g. OI-1, the husband) still wants that same product type  
**When** M-Smart builds the Recommended Package  
**Then** the exclusion is applied **only** to the named member (MI) — the other member's (OI-1's) request for the excluded product type is honored normally  
**And** if the recommendation engine cannot unambiguously resolve which person an exclusion phrase refers to, it asks the FA to clarify rather than guessing.

#### AC8. [Happy Path] Agent explicitly assigns riders per member

**Given** the FA's message explicitly assigns rider types to specific members (e.g. *"CI rider cho vợ, health rider cho con"*)  
**When** M-Smart parses the request  
**Then** it recognizes the Vietnamese/English rider keywords, binds each rider to the specified member after validating eligibility (age, gender, product compatibility), and labels these items *"theo yêu cầu"* to distinguish them from system-suggested additions  
**And** if a named rider is incompatible with the base product, it flags the conflict and offers two options: swap the rider (keep the product) or swap the product (keep the rider).

#### AC9. [Validation] Agent-specified coverage amount, including Vietnamese numeric shorthand

**Given** the FA provides an explicit sum assured for a rider (e.g. *"CI 500 triệu cho vợ"*, *"HCB 200k/ngày cho con"*)  
**When** M-Smart parses the amount  
**Then** it correctly interprets Vietnamese number shorthand (500 triệu = 500,000,000 VND; 200k/ngày = 200,000 VND/day), confirms the interpretation with the FA (*"Tôi hiểu: CI 500.000.000 VND cho vợ. Đúng không?"*), and validates it against the rider's min/max sum assured for that member's age  
**And** the confirmed amount is used exactly as stated in the package — never silently replaced with a system-calculated default.

#### AC10. [Edge Case] Agent names a specific base product or plan code

**Given** the FA explicitly names a base product (e.g. *"dùng RUV10 cho tiết kiệm"*)  
**When** M-Smart evaluates the request  
**Then** it accepts the named product instead of running the default recommendation algorithm, after validating: the product is active, the FA holds the required license, and the product is eligible for MI's age/profile  
**And** if the product has been discontinued, it responds: *"Sản phẩm [X] không còn trong danh mục. Sản phẩm thay thế gần nhất là [Y]. Anh/chị có muốn tiếp tục?"* and waits for confirmation  
**And** the output labels the product: *"Sản phẩm nền: [X] (theo yêu cầu agent)"*.

#### AC11. [Exception Case] Agent requests a rider exclusion that has a dependency conflict

**Given** the FA explicitly requests a rider be removed (e.g. *"bỏ rider tai nạn đi"*), and another active rider (e.g. a WOP waiver) depends on it  
**When** M-Smart processes the exclusion request  
**Then** it warns the FA before removing anything: *"Rider tai nạn là điều kiện để duy trì WOP rider. Nếu loại bỏ, WOP cũng sẽ bị xoá. Xác nhận không?"*  
**And** only after confirmation does it remove the rider, recalculate the package, and mark it *"Đã loại bỏ theo yêu cầu: Rider [X]"*  
**And** it never re-adds the excluded rider in any later regeneration within the same session; if the rider is mandatory for the base product, it blocks the exclusion entirely and explains why.

#### AC12. [Integration Failure] Product/rider validation rule lookup incomplete for a product

**Given** a given product/rider combination needed by the recommendation has no entry (or an incomplete entry) in its ePOS validation rules or official product documentation (provision document / product spec)  
**When** M-Smart attempts to evaluate that rider's eligibility  
**Then** it must **not** fabricate or assume eligibility — it excludes that specific rider from the recommendation and flags it internally for BA follow-up, while still returning the rest of the valid package to the FA.
